Surround Sound 8.1: Channel Reference Table

This document specifies the presumptions the Newer Audio Editor makes during the process of downsampling Surround Sound 8.1 audio into stereo or mono audio. Fun fact: Many surround sound options have their own ".1" variant, which means that there is a separate channel that is able to hold only bass. One example for that situation is explosions, or deep earthquakes.

Channel Index Corresponding Label Is Often Ignored
1 Front Left no
2 Front Right no
3 Front Center (or Center) yes
4 Subwoofer Bass yes
5 Back Left no
6 Back Right no
7 Auxiliary Left (Center Left) yes
8 Auxiliary Right (Center Right) yes

If these conventions are not explicitly followed, the audio editor will not refuse to render the audio. Rather, the audio may sound uneven, rough or telepathic. This is not a critical point to consider, but this may be mandatory if you plan the final product (the exported audio) to be immersive.
