Surround Sound 8.1: Channel Reference Table

This document specifies the presumptions the Newer Audio Editor (now called Searadare) makes during the process of downsampling Surround Sound 8.1 audio into stereo or mono audio. Fun fact: Many surround sound options have a ".1" variant, which means that there is a separate channel that stores bassy audio, which is typically played in subwoofer speakers. This is useful for making sounds like explosions or earthquakes more immersive, realistic or subtly sensational.

Channel Index Corresponding Label Is Often Ignored
1 Front Left no
2 Front Right no
3 Front Center (or Center) yes
4 Subwoofer Bass yes
5 Back Left no
6 Back Right no
7 Auxiliary Left (Center Left) yes
8 Auxiliary Right (Center Right) yes

If these conventions are not explicitly followed, the audio editor will not refuse to render the audio. Instead, the rendered audio may sound uneven, rough or telepathic. This is inconvenient for users who wish to preserve the original environment for the exported audio track.

This is not a critical point to consider, but it may be mandatory if you plan the final product (the exported audio) to be immersive. Struggling to navigate through the audio editor's pages? You would need to look for the tab that's titled "Newer Audio Editor (Searadare)".
